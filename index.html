RULES AND REGULATIONS

Hackathon Rules & Guidelines

PYTHON HACKATHON FRAMEWORK & GUI LIBRARY

  • 1. Tkinter - GUI Library
  • 2. Django - Framework

PYTHON HACKATHON DOMAIN

  • 1. Windows-based
  • 2. Web-based

General Rules

    Eligibility:
  • Open to all I-year students enrolled in the specified academic program.
  • Participants must register before the deadline.

    • Team Composition:
  • Teams of 3-5 members.
  • Teams must be finalized at the start of the event.
  • All members must actively contribute to the project.

    • Code of Conduct:
  • Participants must maintain professionalism and adhere to ethical behavior.
  • Harassment, plagiarism, or any inappropriate behavior will lead to disqualification.

Event Structure

    Hackathon Duration:
  • The event will last 8 hours.
  • Time extensions will not be permitted unless approved by the organizers.

    • Theme & Problem Statements:
  • Problem statements will be disclosed at the start of the event.
  • Teams must choose one problem statement to work on.

    • Deliverables:
    Teams must submit:
  • Source code (on GitHub or as a ZIP file).
  • A short demo video (optional).
  • A README file detailing the project description, features, and usage.

Judging Criteria

    Evaluation Metrics:

  • 1. Functionality (30%): Does the project meet the requirements of the problem statement?
  • 2. Innovation (20%): Is the project creative and unique?
  • 3. UI/UX Design (20%): Is the interface user-friendly and aesthetically pleasing?
  • 4. Code Quality (20%): Is the code structured, modular, and well-documented?
  • 5. Presentation (10%): How effectively did the team demonstrate and explain their project?

    • Judging Panel:
  • Judges will consist of technical experts and mentors.

    • Presentation:
  • Each team will have 5-10 minutes to demonstrate their project and explain its features.

Prizes & Recognitions

    Overall Awards:
  • 1. Champion (1st Place) - ₹5,000 + Certificate of Achievement
  • 2. 1st Runner-Up (2nd Place) - ₹3,000 + Certificate of Achievement
  • 3. 2nd Runner-Up (3rd Place) - ₹2,000 + Certificate of Achievement

    • Special Awards:
  • For Web-Based Solutions - Best UI/UX Design – Cash prize + Certificate
  • For Window-Based Applications - Best Desktop App (GUI/CLI) – Cash prize + Certificate

    • Team Recognitions:
  • 1. Best Team Collaboration – Cash prize + Certificate
  • 2. Most Innovative Idea – Cash prize + Certificate

    • Participation Certificates:
  • All participants will receive certificates of participation.

Technical Rules

    Permitted Technologies:
  • Participants must use Python as the primary programming language.
  • Frameworks and libraries such as Tkinter, Django, and Flask can be used as per the problem statement requirements.

    • Pre-built Code:
  • Reusing small code snippets or templates is allowed if credited appropriately.
  • Projects must not include entirely pre-built solutions.

    • Version Control:
  • Teams are encouraged to use version control platforms like GitHub to manage their code.

Disqualification Rules

    Plagiarism:
  • Any project found to be plagiarized will result in immediate disqualification.

    • Misconduct:
  • Any cheating, tampering with another team’s project, or violating the rules will lead to disqualification.

Support & Assistance

    Mentorship:
  • Mentors will be available during the hackathon to answer technical queries and provide guidance.

    • Technical Issues:
  • Participants must report technical issues (e.g., submission platform problems) to the organizers immediately.


POST-HACKATHON ACTIVITIES

Feedback Survey

Ask participants to share their experiences and suggestions.

Documentation

Share event highlights, winning projects, and learning resources.

Follow-Up

Encourage participants to continue working on their projects or collaborate further.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate in Python Hackathon 2024?

Students who are interested in Python and have experience in Tkinter, Django, or Flask can participate.

Can I participate alone, or do I need a team?

You can participate individually or as a team (max 3 members).

What do I need to bring for the hackathon?

You must bring your own laptop, charger, and any additional accessories required for development.

Can we use pre-built templates or libraries?

No, participants must build projects from scratch within the hackathon duration.

How will the projects be evaluated?

Projects will be judged based on:

  • ✅ Creativity (10 points)
  • ✅ Unique Selling Point (10 points)
  • ✅ Problem-Solving (10 points)
  • ✅ Presentation (10 points)
  • ✅ GUI Application (10 points)
